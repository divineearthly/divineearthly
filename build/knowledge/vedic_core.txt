<s>What are the five elements in Vedic philosophy?
The five elements (Pancha Mahabhuta) in Vedic philosophy are: 1. Prithvi (Earth) - representing solidity and stability, 2. Apas (Water) - representing fluidity and cohesion, 3. Agni (Fire) - representing transformation and energy, 4. Vayu (Air) - representing movement and expansion, and 5. Akasha (Ether/Space) - representing consciousness and the substratum of all existence.</s>

<s>Explain Vedic farming principles.
Vedic farming (Krishi Veda) principles include: 1. Ritu-based cropping following seasonal cycles, 2. Use of Panchagavya (five cow products) as natural fertilizer, 3. Agnihotra ash for natural pest control, 4. Surya-based irrigation timing aligned with solar cycles, 5. Chandra-based planting following lunar phases, 6. Practice of Ahimsa (non-violence) towards all living beings in agriculture.</s>

<s>What are the Vedic mathematics sutras?
The key Vedic mathematics sutras include: Sutra 67 - Nikhilam Navatashcaramam Dashatah (All from 9 and the last from 10) for multiplication, Sutra 68 - Urdhva Tiryagbhyam (Vertically and Crosswise) for general multiplication, and Sutra 21 - Ekadhikena Purvena (By one more than the previous one) for division and squares.</s>

<s>What is Kavach in Vedic tradition?
Kavach (कवच) in Vedic tradition refers to a protective shield or armor. It is a spiritual protection mechanism invoked through mantras and rituals. In technology context, KAVACH represents a security layer that protects Vedic AI systems from corruption and ensures the purity of knowledge transmission.</s>
